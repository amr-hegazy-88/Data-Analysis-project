# Student Depression & Mental Health Analysis
# 📝 Project Overview
This project provides a data-driven exploration into the mental health crisis among students. By analyzing a dataset of over 140,000 records, this Power BI dashboard identifies the core socio-economic and academic factors that contribute to depression and stress, offering a clear roadmap for targeted mental health interventions.
# Dataset used
- <a href = "https://github.com/amr-hegazy-88/Data-Analysis-project/blob/main/Amr%20hegazy.csv">Dataset</a>
# 🎯 Project Objectives
Correlation Mapping: Quantify the relationship between academic pressure and depression status.
Lifestyle Impact: Evaluate how sleep duration and dietary habits influence overall stress levels.
Risk Assessment: Develop a Mental Health Risk Score to identify high-risk student profiles.
Academic Performance: Analyze the trade-off between high CGPA and psychological well-being.
# 📈 Key Metrics & KPIs
Depression Prevalence: Percentage of students screened with positive depression status.
Average Stress Level: A weighted index (0-10) across different student demographics.
Sleep Adequacy: Correlation between "Low Sleep" (<5 hours) and high-stress categories.
Financial & Family Stressors: Tracking the impact of financial instability and family history on mental health.
# ⚙️ The Process
Data Engineering (Power Query): Cleaned and transformed raw CSV data, handled missing values, and created categorical buckets for Sleep, Stress, and Study Load.
Advanced Modeling: Architected a relational model to link demographic data with psychological survey results.
DAX Development: Authored custom measures to calculate the Mental Health Risk Score and compare average stress across different degrees (e.g., MBBS, B.Tech, BSc).
UI/UX Design: Built an intuitive dashboard using slicers for Gender, City, and Degree to allow for granular data exploration.

# 🚀 Key Insights
The Sleep Gap: Students sleeping less than 5 hours are 3x more likely to experience high stress compared to those with 7-8 hours.
Academic Pressure: A direct linear correlation was found between "Heavy Study Load" and "Suicidal Thoughts."
Work-Life Balance: "Poor" work-life balance ratings strongly correlate with lower CGPA, contradicting the myth that overworking leads to better grades.
